## 📌 Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Bike Buyers dataset using Microsoft Excel.  
The goal is to identify patterns and customer characteristics that influence bike purchase decisions.

The analysis focuses on income, age, commute distance, and demographic factors to uncover insights that could support targeted marketing strategies.

---

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains 1,000 customer records with the following columns:

- ID  
- Marital Status  
- Gender  
- Income  
- Children  
- Education  
- Occupation  
- Home Owner  
- Cars  
- Commute Distance  
- Region  
- Age  
- Age Brackets  
- Purchased Bike (Yes/No)

---

## 📊 Dashboard Components

### ✅ Average Income per Purchase (by Gender)

- Males who purchased bikes have the highest average income ($60,124).
- Females who purchased bikes earn more on average than those who did not purchase.
- Customers who purchased bikes generally have higher income levels.

---

### ✅ Customer Commute Distance

Purchase distribution by commute distance:

- 0–1 Miles: Highest purchase count (200 buyers)
- 2–5 Miles: Strong purchase segment (95 buyers)
- Longer commute distances show lower purchase rates.

Insight:
Customers with shorter commute distances are more likely to purchase bikes.

---

### ✅ Customer Age Brackets

- Middle Age group has the highest number of buyers (383).
- Adolescents and older customers show lower purchase rates.
  
Insight:
Middle-aged customers are the primary target market for bike sales.

---

### ✅ Age-Level Purchase Trends

Detailed age-level breakdown shows:
- Peak purchasing activity between ages 30–40.
- Gradual decline in purchase frequency after age 50.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Higher income customers are more likely to purchase bikes.
- Middle-aged individuals represent the largest buyer segment.
- Short commute distance correlates with higher purchase likelihood.
- Gender differences exist in income levels but both genders show strong purchasing patterns in middle age.

---

## 🎯 Business Value

This analysis helps:

- Identify high-probability customer segments.
- Optimize marketing strategies based on age and income.
- Target customers with short commute distances.
- Improve customer segmentation for sales campaigns.
